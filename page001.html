Electronical equipment

"What you need to have on hand in any situation."







Multimeter:

If you go to the toilet in the dark, you’ll end up shitting on the floor.

A multimeter is like a flashlight in the dark; it shows you the voltage, current, resistance, and more of any metal or circuit.

IT IS A MUST-HAVE!

Soldering Iron:

Just like with you and your woman, sometimes things need to be forged together into one flash to work properly.

Without a soldering iron and its kit, it’s impossible to build anything.

IT IS A MUST-HAVE!

Power Supply:

No shit, Sherlock you need power to make motors move.

Whether it's a laptop or a homemade machine, you need a positive and a negative end.

For beginners, an Arduino's 5V will be more than enough.

For those getting serious, invest in a machine that can limit current.

Breadboard + Jumper Cables:

Come on now, you’re not stupid, right?

you knew you’d need cables to work with electricity.

First, buy a good-quality breadboard that’s conductive and firm.
Then get jumper cables and alligator clips.

Components:

If you buy an Arduino kit, you’ll get every cheap, crappy component from China:

buttons, knobs, those flickery fucks, resistors, you name it.

But keep in mind, you’ll need to buy special parts in the future (servo motors, transformers, etc.).

The cheapest way to get these is through sites like Shein, Temu, or AliExpress etc.. The unpaid kids make pretty good resistors.

Protective Gear:

Don’t kill yourself.

I’m harsh, but I want you to succeed.

Dead people can’t build shit.

Always wear gloves and shoes when working with electricity.
You don’t want to end up fried, do you?

Insulation:

Get a pack of heat-shrink tubing to cover exposed wires.
Use a lighter to tighten it up.

It’s cheap. Don’t be a frugal FUCK!

Mechanical equipment

Hammer:

For when things don’t fit.

Welding Machine:

To join metals together—permanently.

Screwdriver:

The classic tool for tightening and loosening screws.

Wrenches:

To grip, tighten, and loosen bolts and nuts.

Angle Grinder (Flex):

To cut, grind, and shape metal or other tough materials.

3D Printer:

The most important tool

when your broke ass makes enough money to buy a good 3D printer, buy a Prusa or Bambu Lab.

DON"T BE A FRUGAL FUCK!

It lets you create the exact plastic parts you need for your machine.
Totally worth the investment.

CNC Machine:

This might be overkill,
but if you’re making agricultural machines, you’ll need to work with steel cutting, shaping, and all that shit.

Minimal Budget to Start

The minimal budget to start building robots is $50.

Buy a $20 Arduino kit.
Use the rest for a multimeter and take a trip to the scrap metal store in your city.
The best engineers started here.

The optimal budget is around $3,000.

With $3,000, you can build a full laboratory capable of producing and manufacturing complex machines.
